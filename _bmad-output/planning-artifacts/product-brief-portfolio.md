# Product Brief: Saad's Automation Portfolio Website

## Executive Summary

Saad is a bachelor's student specializing in workflow automation with n8n and Python. As the automation job market accelerates — 388 n8n-specific roles in Germany alone and climbing — early entrants with documented, practical skills have a significant first-mover advantage. The problem: Saad has real, valuable skills and projects, but no professional channel to surface them to the employers who need them most.

A personal portfolio website solves this by giving employers a compelling, credible, self-updating showcase of his automation work — making it easy to evaluate his skills, see real outcomes, and reach out. This is not just a resume in website form. It is a living proof-of-work platform designed to grow alongside Saad's skills and serve him through his degree, his first Werkstudent role, and beyond.

## The Problem

Employers hiring working students in automation face a noisy signal problem: many candidates claim n8n or Python skills on a CV, but few can demonstrate real, impact-driven work. Meanwhile, Saad — who has already built production-quality workflows in customer support automation, RAG pipelines, and AI chatbots — has no professional home to showcase this work.

A LinkedIn profile or GitHub repository is not enough. Non-technical recruiters cannot parse raw workflow JSON or navigate repository structure. Without a curated, narrative-driven portfolio, Saad's skills remain invisible to the employers best positioned to benefit from them.

The status quo: talented students miss Werkstudent opportunities because they compete on CV bullet points rather than demonstrated outcomes.

## The Solution

A personal portfolio website — clean, fast, and professional — that serves as Saad's automation-focused digital identity:

- **Project showcase**: Each n8n workflow and Python project presented as a case study — what problem it solved, how it works, and what the business impact is — with links to live demos, exported workflow JSON, and GitHub repositories
- **About & skills**: A brief, authentic personal narrative covering background, skills (n8n, Python), languages (German B1, English), and the kind of work he is looking for
- **CV section**: Downloadable resume plus an inline skills summary
- **Contact**: Email form (via Formspree), LinkedIn, and GitHub — visible, accessible, impossible to miss

## What Makes This Different

Most student portfolios are either GitHub profiles (invisible to non-technical recruiters) or template-generated Notion and website-builder pages that fail to signal developer credibility. Saad's portfolio differentiates on three dimensions:

1. **Domain specificity**: Explicitly positioned for automation roles — n8n, Python, AI-augmented workflows — rather than generic developer work. Rare in the Werkstudent candidate pool.
2. **Impact-first storytelling**: Projects documented with business outcomes ("handles X customer queries automatically", "reduces manual processing by Y hours/week"), not just technical descriptions.
3. **Developer-credible medium**: Built with Astro, hosted on Vercel, linked to GitHub — the site itself signals that Saad is a builder, not just a template user.
4. **Compounding asset**: Designed to grow with Saad — new projects slot in naturally, and the site evolves as his career advances.

## Who This Serves

**Primary — Hiring managers and technical recruiters** at startups, SMEs, and consulting firms with automation needs. They review 20–30 candidates, spend under 30 seconds per portfolio, and need to answer one question fast: "Can this person actually do the work?" The portfolio must answer that instantly.

**The owner — Saad** needs a professional channel that converts employer curiosity into interview requests. Success means employers can quickly understand his skills, see real work, and know exactly how to reach him.

## Success Criteria

**Short-term (0–3 months):**
- Site is live with 2–3 polished, fully documented project case studies
- At least one n8n workflow published to n8n.io template library and linked from the site
- At least one Werkstudent interview request directly attributable to the portfolio

**Medium-term (3–12 months):**
- Portfolio grows to 5+ projects as Saad continues building
- GitHub activity graph consistently green (hiring managers check this)
- Site discoverable via search for automation-related terms

**Long-term:**
- Maintained as a living professional profile through the end of Saad's degree and into his first full-time role

## Scope (V1)

**In:**
- Hero / home section (who Saad is, what he does, what he is looking for)
- Projects section with 2–3 case studies: customer support automation, RAG pipeline, AI chatbot
- About section: skills (n8n, Python), languages (German B1, English), personal narrative
- CV section: downloadable PDF + inline skills summary
- Contact section: email form (Formspree), LinkedIn link, GitHub link
- Mobile-responsive design
- Custom domain (e.g. saadkhurram.dev)

**Out for V1:**
- Blog or articles section
- German / English language toggle
- CMS or admin interface
- Analytics dashboard
- Paid backend infrastructure

**Recommended tech stack:**
- Framework: Astro (fast, low JS, free static hosting, developer-credible)
- Styling: Tailwind CSS
- Hosting: Vercel (free tier, GitHub-integrated CI/CD)
- Contact form: Formspree (free tier, no backend required)
- Domain: Custom .dev or .me (low annual cost)

## Vision

In 2–3 years, this site is no longer just a job-hunting tool — it is Saad's professional brand. As his automation expertise deepens and potentially expands toward consulting, freelancing, or specialist roles, the portfolio grows with him. Projects become detailed case studies. Case studies build a reputation. The site that secured his first Werkstudent offer becomes the foundation for whatever comes next.
